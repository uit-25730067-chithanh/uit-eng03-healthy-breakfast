## Phước Thịnh - Health Benefits of a Healthy Breakfast

### English Script

Good evening everyone, and thank you, Hai, for your clear introduction to our topic.

Before I start, let me ask you a quick question:
How many of you had breakfast this morning?
(You can raise your hand or type “yes” in the chat.)
(Pause 2–3 seconds)
Oh, nice! / Hmm, not many… that’s interesting.

Well, today I’m going to talk about the health benefits of having a healthy breakfast.

As you know, after a long night of sleep, our body feels tired and needs energy. A healthy breakfast helps us feel more awake and ready to start the day. Without it, we may feel weak or even a bit dizzy.

Another important point is about weight control. Believe it or not, people who eat breakfast regularly are less likely to eat too much later in the day. When we skip breakfast, we often feel very hungry at lunch and eat more than we need.

You know, breakfast is also a great chance to give our body important nutrients. Foods like fruits, eggs, milk, and whole grains provide vitamins, minerals, and fiber. These nutrients help our immune system stay strong and protect us from getting sick.

Another amazing thing is that breakfast supports our digestion. Eating in the morning helps our metabolism start working earlier. This means our body can use energy more effectively during the day.

In fact, some studies show that people who eat breakfast regularly may have a lower risk of heart disease. So, something as simple as breakfast can bring long-term health benefits.

To sum up, a healthy breakfast gives us energy, helps control our weight, and improves our overall health.

I strongly suggest that everyone make breakfast a daily habit, because it is one of the easiest ways to take care of your body.

That’s all for my part.

Now, I would like to invite Thanh to continue with the next part about the impact on mental and physical performance.

## Chí Thanh - Slide 03 - Impact on Mental and Physical Performance

### OFFICIAL SCRIPT | PART 3: MENTAL & PHYSICAL PERFORMANCE

Speaker: Chí Thanh
Duration: 4 - 5 minutes
Word count: ~430 words
Tone: Storytelling, Professional, Engaging.

**1. INTRODUCTION & INTERACTION**
Thank you, Thịnh, for sharing how breakfast protects our body.

You know, as a Developer, I often see our body as the hardware and our brain as the Operating System. Believe it or not, even the most expensive MacBook Pro cannot run properly if the power supply is unstable.

Quick question for everyone: Raise your hand if you skipped breakfast this morning? (Wait 5s). Wow, that’s a lot of "unpowered systems" in this room! Today, I’ll show you why skipping that "power supply" almost ruined my career.

**2. THE STORY: THE BIGGEST "BUG" IN MY CAREER**
A few years ago, I had a very important meeting with a foreign client. But, after a late party on Sunday, I woke up late. I had no time to eat. I just grabbed a coffee and rushed to the office.

Can you guess what happened? My brain felt like an old computer with only 2GB of RAM. I stared at my slides, but my mind was blank. I started stuttering and forgot my words. I couldn't even explain the logic of my own code!

Because I didn't have enough glucose to fuel my brain, I looked like an amateur. In our English 3 class, we learned that: "It is essential that a developer eat breakfast." I learned this the hard way. It’s not just grammar; it’s a professional rule.

**3. PHYSICAL PERFORMANCE: THE 20% RULE**
Actually, some students think: "I only sit and code, so I don't need much energy." That’s a big mistake. Research shows that the brain consumes 20% of your total energy. Mental work is a high-energy task for your system.

If you skip breakfast, you might try to "overclock" your body with coffee at 8 AM. But by 10 AM, you will face an "Energy Crash." You’ll feel tired and lose your focus. The best developers are not those who work the longest, but those who keep their energy stable.

**4. CONCLUSION & TRANSITION**
To wrap up: Breakfast is the fuel that boots up your system. Don't let a "silly bug" ruin your work just because of an empty stomach. Always remember: "Your code is only as good as your breakfast."

We've seen how missing breakfast lags your system today. But what if this becomes a habit? What is the “Technical Debt” for your health in the long run?

To answer that, let’s welcome Sơn for the next part.

Sơn, the stage is yours!

## Đức Ý - Tips for a Healthy Breakfast

### OFFICIAL SCRIPT | PART : Tips for a Healthy Breakfast

Speaker: Nguyễn Đức Ý
Duration: 4 - 5 minutes

**Block 1: Transition In (Connection)**
First off, a huge thank you to Mr. Son for your insightful sharing on the long-term consequences of poor eating habits. You’ve certainly given us plenty to consider!

Hi everyone, I’m Y. Building on what Mr.Son mentioned about energy levels, I will now dive into some practical ways we can “upgrade” our most important meal of the day: Breakfast.

**Block 2: Main Content (Student-Centered Tips)**
But before we begin, let me ask: do you usually grab a quick bite on your way to work, or do you take the time to eat at home? [Pause & interact]…I see, most of us are in a massive hurry!

We are lucky to live in a food paradise with Phở, Bành mì, and Xôi. However, these aren’t always “student-friendly” for our brains if we don’t choose wisely. Today, I’ll share three simple tips to stay energized.

**First, Balance Your Macronutrients**
Most student breakfasts are too high in carbs, which is the primary reason why we often feel exhausted by 10:00 AM. When your blood sugar spikes and crashes, you lose focus. To fix this, it is essential that every student add more protein and fiber to their morning meal. For example, when ordering Bánh mì, ask for extra cucumber and an additional egg to stay sharp until lunch!

**Second, Rethink Your Drinks**
We often use sweet iced coffee to wake up, but the condensed milk in Cà phê sữa leads to a "sugar crash" later. It is vital that we reduce our daily intake of sweet syrups and artificial flavors. Instead of milk tea, try fresh, unsweetened soy milk. Trust me, your heart and your skin will thank you for it.

**Third, Embrace Local Produce**
Tropical fruits are cheap and fresh here. Instead of buying processed snacks like crackers, grab a banana or a slice of papaya, which are the best natural multivitamin you can find. It’s affordable, fast, and remarkably healthy.

**Block 3: Transition Out (Handover)**
That summarizes my three tips for a better breakfast: add more greens, cut down on sugar, and stick to fresh produce. We now know what to eat, but how do we actually put this into a daily routine?
To clear this up and provide you with a clear action plan, let’s welcome Thanh Hai to the stage. Hai, the floor is yours!
